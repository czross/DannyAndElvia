Travel Details

FLIGHTS

Flights arrive daily to Puerto Vallarta (PVR). It is important to note that this time of year is high season for travel to Sayulita, so we highly recommend booking your flights ASAP. Ana and Sean will begin arriving as early as February 28th and will be staying through March 7th.  We understand that not everyone will be able to join us for the entire week but a weekend in Sayulita is well worth the travel.

Some tips from us:
- In the world of booking flights, it is generally thought that prices are best on Tuesdays and Wednesdays. 
- Note for San Francisco Guests: There are only two airlines that fly non-stop from San Francisco to PVR.  Those are United Airlines and Alaska Airlines. These flights tend to be more expensive but are well worth the convenience.

Time Zone: 
Sayulita is conveniently located in the Central Time Zone. 

Passports: 
Please make sure those passports are renewed! For flights to and from international destinations, including Mexico, government regulations require the use of a passport for entry into, and exit from the United States for U.S. Citizens as well as foreign visitors. ACCOMMODATIONS

Houses and Hotels

Sayulita Bay is approximately 2 miles long, with the north and south ends divided by a river. The south side of the beach is closer to the town (and restaurants and bars), so it's more active and a bit louder. The north end of the beach is more beautiful and serene, yet is only a 10 minute walk to town. The wedding will take place at - Don Pedro's Palapa - on Punta Sayulita at the South side of Sayulita. 

The most popular types of accommodations in Sayulita are private villas and condos. There are no large resorts in Sayulita, but there are a few boutique hotels. Due to the fact that March is a popular travel time for Sayulita, it is important to book EARLY. (How early? Basically now.) Ana and Sean are here to help! We have already done a lot of research for you, so we are happy to help coordinate homes, groups, bookings and answer questions. We can assure that there is something for every preference and budget. 

Please note that most villas require a 50% deposit upfront and may require a minimum number of nights (larger homes are typically 5 or 7). Many villas are also booked on a Saturday - Saturday schedule. The rental and payment processes are reliable and secure.

The boutique hotels in Sayulita are a good option for a couple or a group no larger than 4.  They typically will also require a 50% upfront deposit to secure the booking but will be slightly less expensive than the villas or condos.

A few hotels that we really like: 

  • Villa Amor
  • Hotel Petit Hafa
  • Hotel Vogue
  • Aurinko Bungalows

A complete list of homes and hotels can be found at: www.sayulitalife.com

TRANSPORTATION

From The Airport

Transportation from the airport is safe, easy and affordable. There are three options for transportation to Sayulita from the airport:  bus, taxi, or car service.  

Bus and taxi information can be found here: www.sayulitalife.com/sayulitatransportation.htm

Car service is the way to go in our opinion and is great if you want convenience. The cost is a bit more expensive than a taxi but not unreasonable. They meet you at the airport at your arrival time, provide bottled water and beer for the drive, and will stop for up to an hour at a grocery store for you to pick up supplies for your stay in Sayulita. The cost ends up being about $25 per person for the 45-minute + 1-hour pit stop drive to Sayulita.

Our favorite car service: Diva Tours

Once we know when everyone is arriving and where they are staying we can help to coordinate transportation if needed.

In Sayulita

Sayulita is very walkable but does have some hills. The walk from the north end to the south end of town is about 20 minutes, with the main square located in the center of town. Taxis are common and easy to catch in the town square, or you can call for a pickup from your house.

Golf carts are also a common mode of transportation within the town and can be rented for $50 USD per day here: http://www.sayulitalife.com/pacificcoast
